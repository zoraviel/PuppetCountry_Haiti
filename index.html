A Look Inside Haiti's History with Foreign Powers Through its Presidents.

Haiti is known as being the first slave nation to gain its independence from its captors. However, its sovereignty is still questionable. It is considered the poorest country within the Western Hemisphere and continues to receive aid in the form of donations, military and political advisement. The country shows little sign of economic growth, despite the constant presence of foreign power. From the very start, Haiti has been dealing with "superpower" countries with little gain.

1804–1806 Jean-Jacques-Dessalines

He's considered to be the first president of Haiti and one of the leaders of its first revolution.

1902–1908 Pierre Nord Alexis

Nord Alexis was a powerful member of the military before he became president, and was also accused of being a tool for the U.S. and Europe. The Haitian government passed legislation against Syrian immigrants during this time, preventing them from participating in the market economy.

1915–1934 Occupied Haiti

U.S. Marines landed on Haitian soil on July 28, 1915, and that marked the start of nearly two decades under U.S. control. Pierre Sully, a Haitian soldier guarding the country's military arsenal, famously told the U.S. Marines that they would only entire over his dead body, and that's exactly what they did.

1971–1986 Jean-Claude Duvalier

It would be more fitting to call Duvalier's presidency a dictatorship as he simply continued his father's legacy under the name Baby Doc. He was a lavish president and neglected his tole by giving most of his responsibilities to his advisors.

2017–present Jovenel Moïse

Less text is written about Moise, but the current state of affairs is enough to illustrate how he's been influenced by foreign powers. Currently, there is a major state of unrest within Haiti over several kidnappings happening within the country. Many protests are occurring urging him to resign because of his lack of action against the crimes.

Additonal Information

Haiti has been given a significant amount of foreign aid over the past few years. The U.S. alone has given Haiti millions of dollars in foreign aid, yet the country's economy still suffers greatly. Although there are some good presidents, Haiti has consistently been led by questionable leadership that doesn't seem to have the prosperity of the country in mind.
